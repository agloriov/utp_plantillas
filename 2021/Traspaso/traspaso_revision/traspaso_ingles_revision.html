Important: remember that in some cases, disabled students or students in exceptional situations might require accomodations in order to take their online evaluations. In these cases, we recommend identifying the student's needs, and offering alternatives that fit said needs.

Examples:

    • If a student has motor disabilities and cannot carry out a written essay evaluation, you could allow the student to record themselves reciting their essay, or recommending him assistive technologies that allow them to dictate their essay.
    • If a student has reduced eyesight, and the evaluation requires an image being analyzed, you could offer her an audio alternative narrating or explaining the image; or a transcript with an objective description of the image. You could also make an exception and offer a similar excercise that also tests the same skill (critical analysis) for a different piece (a song, a film, a poem, etc.)
