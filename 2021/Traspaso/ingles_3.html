Important: remember that in some cases, disabled students or students in exceptional situations might require accomodations in order to take their online evaluations. In these cases, we recommend identifying the student's needs, and offering alternatives that fit said needs.

Examples:

    • If a student has motor disabilities and cannot carry out a written essay evaluation, you could allow the student to record themselves reciting their essay, or recommending him assistive technologies that allow them to dictate their essay.
    • If a student has reduced eyesight, and the evaluation requires an image being analyzed, you could offer her an audio alternative narrating or explaining the image; or a transcript with an objective description of the image. You could also make an exception and offer a similar excercise that also tests the same skill (critical analysis) for a different piece (a song, a film, a poem, etc.)

4.Considerations about your participation in the Questions and Introductions Forums

  1. About the Questions Forum

Your student’s participation in the Questions Forum is important. With it, you’ll be able to know the questions they may have throughout the course, their topics of interest and a way to motivate them with whatever they may need. Here are some tips you can use to take advantage of it:

  • Check the Questions Forum every day.
  • Answer the students’ questions assertively and clearly, offering them feedback if they need it. Remember to stay composed and be kind when you reply.
  • Be the first to post on the forum, giving them the following information:
    • Your typical replying schedule, to let them know when they can expect a response from you.
    • A comment explaining that all participation by students is welcome.
    • An encouragement to make the forum a collaborative space, letting them know they too can reply and respond to other students’ questions.
  • Motivate your students to use the forum. You yourself can make questions for them to answer.
  • Avoid copy-pasting the same response to every question.

  1. About the Introduction Forum

Every student’s participation in the Introduction Forum is important, because it will allow them to start getting to know each other, know what their major is, and what motivated them to choose their career. As a teacher, you must motivate students to use this forum. You can use the following tips to do it successfully:

  • Introduce yourself to your students first. You can record yourself and post the video on the forum, giving them some basic information about yourself.
    • Your full name
    • Your profession
    • Some of your work and academic experience
    • Your expectations for the outcome of the course and your students
    • What motivated you to choose your career.

Remember you are making this introduction to allow your students to feel comfortable introducing themselves as well. As such, your speech and body language shouldn’t be too formal, and you should try to be open and approachable, as well as to motivate them to continue moving forward with their professional goals.
